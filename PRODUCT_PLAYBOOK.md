# Tachistoscope Pro Product Playbook

This project is now positioned as a lightweight attention-testing product, not just a classroom demo.

## What Changed

- The app now saves every session into reusable history, not just the latest result.
- Reports carry participant, institution, cohort, and contact metadata.
- The welcome screen includes pricing anchors, a live business dashboard, a leaderboard, and a recent session library.
- The app can be installed as a PWA and used offline after the first load.

## Who Should Pay You

1. Colleges running psychology practicals.
2. Coaching centers offering paid cognitive drills or workshops.
3. Independent trainers, counselors, or labs selling one-off reports.

## Simple Revenue Models

1. Individual report: charge per learner and export PDF/CSV after the session.
2. Cohort package: charge one fee for a batch and include benchmark comparisons.
3. Annual license: sell to institutions with setup, branding, and onboarding.

## Suggested Offer Ladder

1. Starter: one learner, one report, one retest window.
2. Cohort: up to 30 learners, branded exports, benchmark dashboard.
3. Campus: yearly access, faculty onboarding, branded deployment.

## Fastest Path To First Revenue

1. Add your own brand name in the `Institution / Coaching Brand` field and use it in demos.
2. Run 5-10 sample sessions so the dashboard and leaderboard look alive.
3. Export two or three polished reports to use as sales proof.
4. Sell a small paid pilot to one coaching center or one college department before building more.

## Best Next Upgrades

1. Add login and cloud sync so multiple staff members can use the same data.
2. Add Stripe or Razorpay checkout for self-serve purchases.
3. Add faculty dashboards and class-level export bundles.
4. Add branded templates per institution.

## Positioning Line

`Tachistoscope Pro helps labs and coaching centers run browser-based attention assessments with branded reports and cohort analytics.`
