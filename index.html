Psychology Context

What the experiment teaches

Span of attention reflects how many distinct elements can be consciously grasped in one brief exposure. In many settings, the range falls near 4 to 7 items, though familiarity, focus, and exposure duration shift that boundary.

  • Exposure duration: Longer visibility usually supports stronger recall.
  • Stimulus complexity: Words and mixed symbols ask more of perception than isolated letters.
  • Attention control: Momentary concentration shapes both accuracy and order memory.
  • Practice effects: Repeated trials can produce measurable learning gains.
